Overview

This Power BI project analyzed movie data from 1914 to 2023, uncovering insights on film production trends, ratings, and audience engagement over more than a century. Key analyses included critical reviews for the top 50 films by audience size, the most prolific studios, distribution of films by rating, and theatrical release trends over time. Additionally, the project examined genre-based release counts, average critic and audience scores, and the most prolific directors and actors, comparing their average ratings. Interactive visualizations, tooltips, and drill-throughs allowed for dynamic exploration, with a refined color scheme enhancing readability.

Project Based Findings

Having multiple pages with specific data visualizations based on Genres, Directors, Actors, and Writers—along with seamless navigation between them—will allow users to easily find the information they need, whether they are looking for broad trends, specific individuals, or filtering by year and other criteria.

Having a tooltip feature to provide additional information about a movie without requiring users to switch pages or take up extra space keeps the design clean and user-friendly. The tooltips appear only when needed, ensuring a clutter-free interface while still offering deeper insights at a glance.

Personal Changes I made

To make the project stand out in my portfolio and enhance data visualization, I moved away from the default dark purple color scheme and adopted a brighter blue palette. This not only improves visual contrast and readability but also makes key insights more engaging and accessible for viewers. Additionally, this design change provided me with hands-on troubleshooting experience, allowing me to refine visual elements and optimize the report for better user engagement.
